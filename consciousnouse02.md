3D Time Framework — Comprehensive Summary (t1 / t2 / t3, Containers, Sensors, Experiments, Synergy)
This document summarizes the conceptual framework, physical analogies, experimental ideas, and theoretical extensions discussed so far. It is designed as a complete reference for further exploration with an LLM.

1. Core 3D Time Framework
1.1 Three Dimensions of Time
- t1 — Linear Flow
- The standard forward arrow of time.
- Light exists purely in t1 (no internal time, no persistence).
- t2 — Intent / Steering / Choice
- Represents directional bias toward possible futures.
- Analogous to selecting among parallel paths or world‑lines.
- t3 — Observation / Persistence / Awareness
- Represents stabilization of a state across time.
- Mass arises from looping or oscillation in t3.
- Observation “locks in” a coordinate in t3.
1.2 Light, Mass, Gravity
- Light: pure t1 flow, no internal persistence.
- Mass: t1 + t3 looping; persistence through temporal oscillation.
- Gravity: emergent effect of mass looping through t3.
- Observation: stabilization of a temporal coordinate.
- Intent: active steering along t2.
- Life: unification of t2 (steering) and t3 (persistence).
- Bio‑Field: causal pressure created when observation and intent synchronize.

2. Unified t2/t3 Concept — The Coherence Axis
Originally t2 and t3 were treated as separate. We merged them into a single concept:
2.1 Coherence Axis
A living container’s fundamental temporal property that simultaneously:
- stabilizes its internal state (t3‑like), and
- directs its evolution (t2‑like).
Observation and intent are not separate processes; they are two expressions of one underlying mechanism.
2.2 Properties of the Coherence Axis
- Stability: persistence of identity.
- Directionality: bias toward future states.
- Coupling: inseparability of sensing and acting.
2.3 Living Container Definition
A living container is:
A self‑stabilizing, self‑steering temporal system whose observation and intent are unified into a single coherence process.


3. Synergy Between Containers
3.1 Definition
Synergy is coherence coupling between multiple containers.
3.2 Types of Synergy
- Resonant Synergy: coherence axes reinforce each other (trust, alignment).
- Dissonant Synergy: coherence axes destabilize each other (conflict).
- Emergent Synergy: a new higher‑order container forms (group identity).
- Nested Synergy: containers within containers (cells → tissues → organisms → families → cultures).
3.3 Framework Interpretation
Synergy is not metaphorical; it is a temporal resonance phenomenon where multiple coherence axes partially merge.

5. Biological Analogues
5.1 Why Add Life?
Adding biological components introduces:
- internal memory
- metabolic drift
- micro‑structure evolution
- emergent behavior
- history dependence
5.2 Options
- Greek yogurt: safe, biologically rich, but opaque.
- Probiotics in saline: translucent, active, easy to handle.
- Dilute nutrients: allow slow metabolic activity.
- No strong magnetic ions: aligns better with real biology.
5.3 Biological Magnetism
- Most organisms are not strongly magnetic.
- Magnetoreception exists (cryptochrome, radical pairs, magnetite).
- Biology interacts with magnetic fields via weak, quantum‑level effects, not bulk magnetism.
