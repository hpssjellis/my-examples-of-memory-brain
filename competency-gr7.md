1. Math: The Quantitative BrainCategory A: Operational Fluency (The "Engine")
    1. Sub 1: Basic Arithmetic: Speed and accuracy with $+$, $-$, $\times$, $\div$.
    1.. Sub 2: Number Flexibility: Understanding place value (tens, hundreds) and rounding.
    1. Sub 3: Integer Logic: Working with negative numbers (the "opposite" direction).
1. Category B: Proportional Reasoning (The "Lens")
    1. Sub 1: Fractions & Decimals: Seeing parts of a whole as numbers.
    1. Sub 2: Ratio & Rate: Comparing two different things (e.g., $km/h$ or price per gram).
    1.. Sub 3: Percentages: Converting relationships into a base-100 scale for comparison.
1. Category C: Algebraic & Spatial Logic (The "Blueprint")
    1. Sub 1: Patterning: Identifying the "rule" in a sequence of numbers or shapes.
    1. Sub 2: Equality & Variables: Balancing equations and isolating $x$.
    1. Sub 3: Geometric Visualization: Calculating area/volume and understanding 3D space.
1.  English: The Verbal BrainCategory A: Mechanics & Structure (The "Skeleton")
    1. Sub 1: Decoding & Spelling: The ability to recognize and form words correctly.
    1. Sub 2: Grammar & Syntax: Building logical sentences (subject-verb agreement).
    1. Sub 3: Punctuation & Flow: Using markers (commas, periods) to control the pace of an idea.
1. Category B: Comprehension & Synthesis (The "Muscle")
    1. Sub 1: Vocabulary in Context: Determining meaning without a dictionary.
    1. Sub 2: Main Idea Extraction: Summarizing long passages into short, "key" points.
    1. Sub 3: Sequencing: Ordering events or arguments in a logical timeline.
1. CategoryC: Critical Analysis (The "Brain")
    1. Sub 1: Inference: Reading "between the lines" to find hidden meaning.
    1. Sub 2: Perspective & Bias: Identifying who is talking and why they might be biased.
    1. Sub 3: Evidence-Based Claims: Backing up an opinion with a specific fact from the text.
